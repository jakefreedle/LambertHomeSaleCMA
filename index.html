Your Property in Context

A Solid Acreage Property in a Competitive Submarket

The Parker acreage market in this price band is active and well-supplied with quality horse properties right now. Before we talk numbers, it is worth being honest about where your property fits in the field — what it competes well on, and where buyers will apply a discount.

What Works in Your Favor

Natural light and open layout — The vaulted great room with skylights creates a genuinely bright, spacious feel that photographs well and shows exceptionally well. This is a home that breathes, and buyers respond to it immediately. It punches above the square footage on paper.

Hardwood and quality laminate flooring — Wood floors on the main level and quality laminate in the family room give the home a warm, finished feel throughout. This is a meaningful differentiator from competing properties still working with dated carpet.

Smart bedroom layout — Three bedrooms share the same level, with a sizable primary suite featuring a large walk-in closet positioned away from the secondary bedrooms. That separation is something families specifically look for and rarely find in this floor plan type.

Walkout basement — The finished walkout adds real flexibility and usable square footage. It functions as a true second living area, not just storage, and the natural light from the walkout makes a meaningful difference in how it feels versus a standard below-grade basement.

Mountain views — East-facing front range views add meaningful appeal and are marketable. They will not single-handedly move the price ceiling, but they move buyers emotionally during showings, and that matters.

Horse-ready amenities — 2-stall barn with tack room and loafing shed, RA-2 zoning, fenced acreage. The property is set up for horses and delivers the lifestyle buyers in this market are seeking.

No HOA — In a market where many competing properties carry association fees, this is a tangible financial advantage for buyers who want flexibility and lower carrying costs.

Where Buyers Will Apply a Discount

Above-grade SF is the number that hurts most. At 1,647 SF above grade, you are meaningfully smaller than most of the active competition. Many comps offer 1,700 to 2,400 SF above grade. Buyers who have toured the neighborhood will feel the difference, even if the finished walkout basement brings total finished SF to a competitive 3,211.

Kitchen and bathrooms are in line with the neighborhood average — but the properties achieving the highest prices in this comp set have fully updated kitchens and bathrooms. Buyers comparison-shopping will notice the gap between this property and the fully refreshed listings and factor it into their offer.

The barn is a plus, but buyers at this price point expect more. The 2-stall setup is genuinely horse-ready and functional. However, the properties commanding top prices in this neighborhood tend to have a more substantial barn or dedicated workshop space. For many buyers in this range, more barn or a real workshop is on their wish list — and this property does not fully satisfy that. The barn checks the horse box without delivering the working infrastructure that premium buyers are hunting for.

The northern property line is a real concern. The adjacent property to the north presents a significant visual challenge. It reads more like a storage yard than a rural neighbor, and buyers touring the property will see it. In an acreage market where privacy and open space are core to the value proposition, this is a discount factor that is difficult to price around entirely. It will give some buyers pause and narrows the buyer pool to those willing to look past it or screen for it with landscaping over time.

3.37 acres is solid, but not exceptional in this peer group. Several of the top-priced comps offer more acreage and more separation from neighbors. In a market where land means privacy, a little more distance goes a long way.

The Pattern Worth Noting

The properties that moved quickly — 42226 Thunderhill (3 days), 42146 Vista Ridge (4 days), 2104 Helen Court (21 days) — were priced correctly from day one. The ones that sat — 2127 Helen Court (130 days), 1280 Stockholm Way (61 days), 723 Brussels Drive (70 days) — were overpriced at launch or had a specific deficiency buyers heavily discounted. Right pricing generates urgency. Wrong pricing generates silence, and eventually a lower sale price anyway.

Before You List

Recommended Steps to Maximize Your Position

A targeted pre-list investment in presentation will meaningfully impact both sale price and days on market. Every item below is chosen because buyers in this price range will notice it. None of these require a full renovation — the goal is to present the home in a way that removes objections before buyers can form them.

1

Professional Photography — Lead With the Light and the Land

Natural light and acreage sell this property. Listing photos need to capture the vaulted great room, the skylights, the mountain views, and the land. Buyers in this price range make their showing list based on photography — this is not the place to cut corners.

2

Staging — Cleanup, Declutter, and Repaint

No furniture rental is needed. This is about editing what you already have: remove most items from the walls so rooms breathe and read larger, declutter surfaces and closets, and repaint the entire interior in a consistent fresh neutral. A clean, unified palette creates the move-in-ready impression that buyers at this price point expect. This is one of the highest-return investments before listing.

3

Refinish Kitchen Cabinetry

The kitchen is the room buyers spend the most time evaluating. Refinished cabinetry in a fresh, contemporary color combined with the existing granite will close a meaningful portion of the gap between this kitchen and the fully updated kitchens in competing listings. This is a fraction of the cost of full replacement and delivers strong visual impact in photos and in person.

4

Granite Piece in the Primary Bath

Bathrooms are the second room buyers evaluate most critically. Adding a granite vanity top to the primary bath brings it in line with what competing properties offer at this price point and removes a visible objection during showings. Small investment, outsized effect on buyer perception.

5

Deep Clean and Professional Carpet Cleaning

A full deep clean of the entire home combined with professional carpet cleaning is non-negotiable before listing in this price range. Clean homes show better, photograph better, and signal to buyers that the property has been well maintained. Buyers notice, and so do their agents.

6

Price It Right the First Time

The data is clear: properties priced correctly in this neighborhood sell in under 30 days. Properties that are overpriced sit for 60 to 130 days, accumulate days-on-market stigma, and typically close below where a well-priced listing would have landed. In a market with active competition including a coming-soon at $924k, you want to be the compelling value — not the listing that buyers start wondering about.
