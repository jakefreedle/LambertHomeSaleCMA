Your Property in Context

A Solid Acreage Property in a Competitive Submarket

The Parker acreage market in this price band is active and well-supplied with quality horse properties right now. Before we talk numbers, it's worth being honest about where your property fits in the field — what it competes well on, and where buyers will apply a discount.

✅ What Works in Your Favor

Ranch layout with walkout basement — One-level living with full finished lower level is genuinely appealing to a wide buyer pool. No stairs on the main floor is a real differentiator.

Mountain views — East-facing front range views add meaningful appeal. Not a massive value driver by itself, but it's real and it's marketable.

Horse amenities — 2-stall barn with tack room and loading shed, RA-2 zoning, fenced acreage. The property is genuinely horse-ready, not just zoned for it.

No HOA — In a market where many competing properties have association fees, this is a tangible advantage for buyers who want flexibility.

Well, septic, gravel road — Lower carrying costs than municipal utilities. Buyers shopping this price range understand and appreciate this.

Two fireplaces — Gas in the living room, wood stove in the basement family room. That's a lifestyle feature buyers in this market actively look for.

⚠️ Where Buyers Will Apply a Discount

Above-grade SF is the number that hurts most. At 1,647 SF above grade, you're significantly smaller than most of the active competition. Many comps offer 1,700–2,400 SF above grade. Buyers who've toured the neighborhood will feel the difference even if the finished basement brings total SF to a competitive 3,211.

2-stall barn vs. the neighborhood — Your immediate neighbor at 42226 Thunderhill closed at $849k with a 5-stall pole barn of approximately 2,000 SF. Buyers who've seen that property will compare directly.

Average condition and tree coverage — Middle of the road on both counts means you can't command the premium that fully updated or exceptionally landscaped properties achieve. Some competing listings present as genuinely move-in ready and recently refreshed.

3.37 acres — Respectable, but several comps are on 3.75–5+ acres. Buyers looking for maximum land will look elsewhere first.

Getting to Market

What I'd Recommend Before You List

1

Professional Photography — Prioritize the Views and the Land

Mountain views sell this property. The listing photos need to capture the front range views prominently, the acreage, the barn, and the vaulted great room with skylights. Buyers shopping this price point online make their showings list based on photography — this is not the place to cut costs.

2

Lean Into the Horse Property Story

The barn, tack room, loading shed, fencing, and RA-2 zoning need to be front and center in the marketing. There's a specific buyer — the equestrian-minded purchaser who's been searching for exactly this — and they need to find this listing immediately. We market to that buyer directly, not generically.

3

Address Any Visible Deferred Maintenance Before Photos

At average condition, small things matter more than they would in a freshly renovated home. Fresh exterior touch-up paint, a clean organized barn, mowed acreage, and decluttered interiors will not add value directly — but they prevent buyers from discounting. First impressions on acreage properties are formed at the end of the driveway.

4

Price It Right the First Time

The data is clear: properties priced correctly in this neighborhood sell in under 30 days. Properties that are overpriced sit for 60–130 days, accumulate DOM stigma, and often close below where a well-priced listing would have landed. In a market with active competition including a coming-soon at $924k, you want to be the best value, not the stubborn listing.
